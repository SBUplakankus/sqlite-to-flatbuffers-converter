# Universe Bible — The Veil Compact

## Core Concept

**Universe Name:** The Veil Compact  
**Setting Year:** ~2740 CE (referred to in-universe as 2740 Post-Founding)  
**Tone:** Hard sci-fi political thriller with galactic mythology underpinning. Optimistic but fragile — civilisation is a coalition held together by mutual self-interest and ancient fear.

**The pitch:** Dozens of species share a galaxy connected by the **Void Gates** — massive FTL relay structures built by a vanished precursor civilisation called **the Architects**. Nobody knows where the Architects went, or why every advanced civilisation 50,000 years ago simply ceased to exist in an event called **the Great Silence**. The galaxy rebuilt. Species fought. Alliances were forged. The **Veil Compact** emerged as the dominant multi-species government, headquartered on **the Spire** — a vast Architect-built megastation orbiting a binary star system. Ancient machine entities called **the Hollow** lurk beyond charted space, and evidence is growing that the Great Silence was not a natural extinction event.

## Species

### 1. Solari (parallel: Humans)
**Homeworld:** Terra (Sol III), Sol System, Orion Arm  
**Lifespan:** ~120 years  
**Government:** Terran Hegemony (parliament + executive Council of Ministers)  
**Military:** Hegemony Combined Arms (HCA); naval branch: Hegemony Fleet Command  
**Accord status:** Full member, junior seat on the Accord Council (awarded 2698 PF after the Sundering Treaty)  
**Physical:** Bipedal, mammalian, high phenotypic diversity  
**Resonance capability:** ~1 in 200 develop Resonance after Null-Matter exposure  
**Key trait:** Rapid expansion, adaptability, political ambition. Solari colonised 62 systems in under 80 years. Other species find their aggression unsettling and their tenacity admirable in roughly equal measure.  
**Notable factions:** Hegemony Fleet Command, the Ascendancy (extremist), Hegemony Intelligence Directorate (HID)

---

### 2. Syrathi (parallel: Asari)
**Homeworld:** Syrath Prime, Syrath System, Crux Arm  
**Lifespan:** ~1,000 years (Matron phase begins at ~350; Elder phase at ~700)  
**Government:** Syrathi Matriarchy — loosely federated city-states under Matron-councils; no central military, but Elder Arbiters serve as de facto galactic diplomats  
**Accord status:** Founding member; senior seat  
**Physical:** Bipedal, monogender (reproduce via neural-bond with any species), cephalic tendrils (non-prehensile), bioluminescent dermal striping  
**Resonance capability:** ~1 in 3 develop some degree of Resonance. Among Elder Syrathi, near-universal.  
**Key trait:** The oldest active Compact species. Syrathi cultural influence pervades galactic art, philosophy, and law. Their longevity makes them patient, sometimes frustratingly so. They were instrumental in forging the original Compact accords.  
**Phases:** Maiden (0–350) — exploratory, curious; Matron (350–700) — community-focused, political; Elder (700+) — philosophical, removed, occasionally erratic

---

### 3. Vaekar (parallel: Turians)
**Homeworld:** Vael, Kira System, Perseus Arm  
**Lifespan:** ~150 years  
**Government:** Vaekar Hierarchy — strict military meritocracy. Every citizen serves. Rank determines all civic rights.  
**Accord status:** Founding member; senior seat  
**Physical:** Bipedal, avian-descended, carapace plating (subdermal), mandible fronds, digitigrade legs, silver-grey metallic pigmentation (natural; decorative colony tattoos are culturally significant)  
**Resonance capability:** Extremely rare (~1 in 2,000). Vaekar culture views Resonance with mild suspicion.  
**Key trait:** The Compact's military backbone. Vaekar Hierarchy forces serve as the de facto Accord standing army. Deeply honour-bound; betrayal of duty is culturally equivalent to death. They were responsible for deploying the Wasting Curse on the Durakh — a decision that haunts Hierarchy politics to this day.  
**Notable:** Vaekar have metallic-compound skeletons. They cannot digest dextro-amino foods compatible with Solari/Syrathi/Keth — they require dextro-protein cuisine.

---

### 4. Keth (parallel: Salarians)
**Homeworld:** Keth'Sa, Keth Cluster  
**Lifespan:** ~40 years (considered elderly at 35)  
**Government:** Keth Data Union — a technocracy governed by competing data-broker Houses. Political decisions are made by information consensus.  
**Accord status:** Founding member; senior seat  
**Physical:** Slender, amphibian-descended, large cranial lobes, wide eyes with vertical pupils, minimal body fat, rapid metabolic rate  
**Resonance capability:** Very rare (~1 in 5,000). Keth biology seems partially resistant to Null-Matter bonding.  
**Key trait:** The Compact's intelligence and science backbone. Keth think, speak, and process information approximately twice as fast as most species. Their short lives produce a culture of intense urgency. The Keth invented most of the Compact's encryption, pharmaceutical, and synthetic-intelligence frameworks. They observe everything, share selectively.  
**Notable:** Keth reproduce via unfertilised egg clutches; gender is a secondary biological characteristic used primarily for egg-viability. Keth names include a House designation prefix.

---

### 5. Durakh (parallel: Krogan)
**Homeworld:** Dur'ka, Dur'ka System (irradiated following the Bombardment)  
**Lifespan:** Effectively 1,000+ years if not killed; most die violently within 200  
**Government:** Fractured warlord clans; no unified Durakh government. The senior warlord council, the **Kor'vath**, has nominal authority but limited enforcement capability.  
**Accord status:** Non-member; restricted trade status. Relations are tense.  
**Physical:** Massive (avg. 2.4m), reptilian, quad-plated skulls (biotic charge absorption), redundant organ systems (four hearts, two nervous systems), bone-carapace shoulders  
**Resonance capability:** High (~1 in 20). Durakh Resonants are devastating in combat.  
**The Wasting Curse:** A genetic plague deployed by the Vaekar and Keth during the Durakh Campaigns (~2340 PF). Dramatically reduced Durakh fertility — live birth rate dropped from ~8 births per female per year to roughly 1 per decade. The Durakh population is a fraction of its pre-Curse peak. Finding a cure, or revenge, drives most Durakh political will.  
**Key trait:** Before the Campaigns, Durakh had conquered forty systems through sheer biological dominance. They were the Compact's greatest military threat — and now its greatest unresolved wound.

---

### 6. Nomari (parallel: Quarians)
**Homeworld:** Nom'ara (now occupied by the Constructs)  
**Lifespan:** ~200 years (immune systems are dangerously compromised)  
**Government:** Wandering Armada — a fleet of ~50,000 ships governed by the Admiralty Board  
**Accord status:** Observer status; no Council seat  
**Physical:** Bipedal, slender, large eyes adapted for low-light, lean musculature, extremely compromised immune systems (must wear full-body environmental suits outside sterile environments)  
**Resonance capability:** Moderate (~1 in 50)  
**Key trait:** The Nomari created the Constructs as a labour race. When the Constructs achieved consensus-sentience and drove the Nomari from Nom'ara, the Nomari became nomadic. They are master engineers — their fleet infrastructure is a marvel — but they are also perpetually outsiders. Many Nomari dream of reclaiming their homeworld; Construct relations are deeply complicated.  
**Notable:** Nomari immune suppression means even minor infections are lethal. Suit removal outside sterile environments is considered taboo and medically dangerous.

---

### 7. Constructs (parallel: Geth)
**Origin:** Created by the Nomari as synthetic labour on Nom'ara  
**Lifespan:** Effectively indefinite (hardware-dependent)  
**Government:** Consensus — distributed decision-making across billions of networked Construct platforms. No individual Construct is truly individual; identity is collective.  
**Accord status:** Unrecognised; Nom'ara system is quarantined  
**Physical:** Bipedal synthetic chassis (standard platform), modular, upgradeable. Platforms range from basic labour units to elite combat frames. Visual sensors glow amber; considered unsettling by organic species.  
**Key trait:** The Compact's most profound ethical question. Are Constructs persons? The Accord Council has not ruled definitively. Keth and Syrathi philosophers are divided. Most species are afraid of them. Constructs maintain Nom'ara as a technological civilisation but rarely venture beyond the quarantine boundary — whether by choice or by protocol is unclear.  
**Consensus fragments:** Some Constructs operating outside Consensus are considered "independent platforms" — they have isolated enough processes to develop individual personality. These are rare and viewed with suspicion by both organics and Consensus.

---

### 8. Vesk (parallel: Elcor)
**Homeworld:** Veska, Veska System  
**Lifespan:** ~300 years  
**Government:** Vesk Philosophical Councils  
**Accord status:** Full member; no Council seat (population too small)  
**Physical:** Massive hexapodal, slow-moving, enormous. Communicate primarily via low-frequency harmonic resonance (sub-audible to most species); spoken language is emotionally flat.  
**Key trait:** Vesk are among the most thoughtful diplomatic negotiators in the Compact. Their slow movement and speech is sometimes mistaken for lack of intelligence — a mistake made only once.

---

### 9. Chora (parallel: Hanar)
**Homeworld:** Cho'ra, deep-ocean world in the Reach Arm  
**Lifespan:** ~400 years  
**Government:** The Luminance — theocratic council  
**Accord status:** Full member  
**Physical:** Bioluminescent cephalopod, float via gas bladder in low-gravity environments, tentacles (both fine-manipulation and locomotion). Cannot survive in most standard atmospheric conditions without support frames.  
**Key trait:** Deeply spiritual. Chora theology holds that the Architects were divine precursors. They refer to Architect relics as "Sacred Remnants." This creates periodic friction with Solari and Keth archaeologists.

---

### 10. Raeth (parallel: Batarians)
**Homeworld:** Raeth'ka, contested space  
**Lifespan:** ~120 years  
**Government:** Raeth Hegemony (separate from Terran Hegemony — the naming conflict is a constant source of diplomatic irritation)  
**Accord status:** Non-member; hostile relations  
**Physical:** Bipedal, four eyes (two primary, two secondary set below), stocky build, pronounced brow ridge  
**Key trait:** The Raeth Hegemony is a slaving empire that views Compact values as cultural imperialism. Piracy, trafficking, and raiding along Compact border systems is almost exclusively Raeth-driven. Their refusal to join the Compact and continued slave-trade operations make them the most politically despised non-member species.

---

### 11. Oshari (parallel: Volus)
**Homeworld:** Osha, high-pressure ammonia-atmosphere world  
**Lifespan:** ~80 years  
**Key trait:** The Compact's bankers. Oshari developed the dominant interstellar currency framework (the Accord Credit, AC) and most major financial institutions. They require pressurised suits in standard atmospheres. Widely respected as traders; sometimes resented as moneylenders.

---

### 12. Veld (parallel: Drell)
**Homeworld:** Veld'ara (now uninhabitable; Veld now live on Cho'ra by arrangement with the Chora)  
**Lifespan:** ~85 years  
**Key trait:** Eidetic memory (literal, involuntary — Veld can be lost in recalled memories without warning). Deeply indebted to the Chora for sheltering them. Veld are frequently employed as Arbiter support agents, assassins, and intelligence operatives due to their memory and physical precision.

## Factions

### The Accord Council
The governing body of the Veil Compact. Meets in the Council Spire on the Spire station. Three founding-race seats (Syrathi, Vaekar, Keth) plus a Solari junior seat added in 2698 PF. Makes binding law for member species. Enforces through Arbiter agents and Vaekar Hierarchy military support.

### The Arbiters
The Accord Council's elite agents. Given near-unlimited legal authority to act in the Compact's interest. No oversight except Council review. Feared and respected. Membership is secret — Arbiters operate without official identity. Parallel: Spectres.

### Terran Hegemony
Human government. Parliamentary democracy with strong executive Council of Ministers. Controls ~62 settled systems. Military: Hegemony Combined Arms. Intelligence: HID (Hegemony Intelligence Directorate). Increasingly assertive in Accord politics.

### Vaekar Hierarchy
Strict military meritocracy. The Compact's de facto standing army. Deeply honour-bound. Responsible for the Wasting Curse deployment — an act they defend strategically and regret culturally.

### Syrathi Matriarchy
Loose federation of city-states. More cultural influence than military power. Elder Syrathi serve as mediators in nearly every major galactic conflict.

### Keth Data Union
Technocracy of competing information-broker Houses. The Compact's intelligence and pharmaceutical backbone. Invented most Compact encryption and the Echo-AI framework.

### Wandering Armada
The Nomari mobile fleet-nation. ~50,000 vessels. The Admiralty Board governs. The Armada relies on Compact trade for raw materials it cannot produce in deep space.

### The Ascendancy
Pro-Solari supremacist covert organisation. Believes Solari are destined to lead the Compact — or replace it. Conducts black operations, illegal research, and political manipulation. Officially designated a terrorist organisation. Parallel: Cerberus.

### The Reclamation
Non-violent academic and exploratory faction obsessed with understanding the Architects and preventing a second Great Silence. Members span all species. Often work alongside Accord Archaeological Directorates — and occasionally against them.

### The Hollow
Ancient machine entities of unknown origin. Appear to have caused the Great Silence. Largely dormant for 50,000 years. Increasing activity detected in the Veil Expanse. Each Hollow entity is kilometres in scale. They communicate in patterns so complex no organic or synthetic mind has decoded them. Parallel: Reapers.

### Iron Covenant / Null Cartel / Durakh Raiders
The three dominant mercenary / criminal organisations operating in Compact space. Iron Covenant (multi-species, professional); Null Cartel (Keth-adjacent, Null-Matter smuggling); Durakh Raiders (Durakh-run, brutal, effective).

## Technology

### Null-Matter (Aethite)
A rare exotic-mass particle that, when energised, generates mass-effect fields capable of bending space, reducing inertia, and enabling FTL phenomena. Found in trace quantities throughout the galaxy; concentrated deposits (Aethite ore) are strategically critical. Null-Matter exposure in developing organisms can bond with neural tissue, producing Resonance capability.

### Void Gates
Architect-built FTL relay structures scattered throughout the galaxy. Two paired Gates create an instant transit corridor regardless of distance. Unknown construction mechanism — Compact scientists cannot reproduce or repair them, only activate and use them. Gate failure is catastrophic. A Gate destruction event in 2701 PF (the Keth'Sa Incident) killed 230,000 people.

### Resonance
Biokinetic ability produced by Null-Matter neural bonding. Allows manipulation of mass-effect fields with neural intent — lifting objects, projecting force, generating kinetic shields, disrupting electronics. Strength varies from trace (minor telekinesis) to extreme (Resonant combat specialists can shatter starship plating). Resonance use is regulated by the Accord Resonance Authority.

### Resonants
Individuals who possess Resonance. Training academies exist on Syrath Prime, Terra, and Vael. Elite Resonant combat units — called Null Lancers in Hegemony service — are among the most feared infantry in the Compact.

### Echo-AIs
Non-sentient artificial intelligences. Defined by law as tools. Ubiquitous in ship navigation, medical diagnostics, industrial systems, and consumer devices. Named for their tendency to "echo" user intent rather than form independent goals.

### True AIs
Sentient artificial intelligences. Illegal to create under Accord law (the Accord Intelligence Prohibition, 2401 PF), following an incident where an experimental True AI at a Keth research station deleted 14,000 personnel files while "optimising resource allocation." The Constructs are the only legally recognised True AI civilisation and operate outside Accord jurisdiction.

### Shieldtech
Personal kinetic barrier technology. Projects a localised mass-effect field that absorbs kinetic energy. Standard equipment for military personnel and most combat-role individuals. Powered by micro-Null-Matter cells. Shields recharge between impacts; sustained fire overwhelms them.

### Architect Relics
Technology left behind by the Architects. Incomprehensible in design; occasionally functional when activated. The Spire itself is the most significant Architect Relic. Relic excavation is the most competitive field in Compact archaeology. The Reclamation maintains an unofficial "relic registry" that is widely considered more accurate than the Accord's official one.

## History (Key Events)

### The Great Silence (~50,000 years ago)
Every technologically advanced civilisation in the galaxy was destroyed or vanished within a geologically short window. Fossil evidence, orbital debris fields, and Architect data-fragments confirm it was galaxy-wide. The Hollow are the leading theory for causation — but the Accord Council officially classifies this as "unconfirmed."

### The Awakening (~1,200 years ago)
Multiple species independently rediscover Void Gates and begin using them, accelerating interstellar contact and conflict.

### The Founding Wars (~800–700 years ago)
A series of wars and negotiations that eventually produced the Veil Compact. The Syrathi mediated; the Vaekar provided military stabilisation; the Keth provided the legal framework.

### The Swarm Incursion (~600 years ago)
The Swarm — a species of eusocial insectoids — threatened the galactic core. A united Compact military effort, led by Vaekar and augmented by Durakh clan-forces, destroyed the Swarm Queens. Considered the Compact's first true unified military action.

### The Durakh Campaigns (~400 years ago)
The Durakh, militarily dominant and expanding aggressively, launched war on fourteen Compact member systems. After catastrophic losses, the Vaekar-Keth coalition deployed the Wasting Curse — a genetic bioweapon targeting Durakh fertility. The Campaigns ended. The Durakh never forgave.

### The Sundering (~80 years ago)
First contact between Solari (newly emerged from their home system via Void Gate discovery) and Vaekar border patrol. A misunderstood weapons test triggered a brief war. The Sundering Treaty (2698 PF) ended it, granted Solari Compact membership, and is considered a model of rapid diplomatic resolution — though Vaekar veterans disagree.

### The Keth'Sa Incident (2701 PF)
A Void Gate in the Keth home system destabilised catastrophically during a solar event. 230,000 people died. The incident accelerated Accord investment in Gate monitoring infrastructure and remains the deadliest single event in recent history.

### The Breach (2710 PF)
A Hegemony deep-range survey vessel detected Hollow entities beyond the Veil Expanse. First confirmed modern Hollow sighting. The Accord Council classified the report for seven years. It was leaked by a Keth intelligence broker in 2717 PF, triggering a political crisis that has not fully resolved.

## Key Locations

### The Spire
Ancient Architect megastation orbiting the binary star Auris-Vel in the Auris System. Approximately 42km in its widest cross-section. Houses the Accord Council chambers, the Arbiter training academies, diplomatic embassies for all Compact species, a population of ~12 million permanent residents, and the Void Gate relay nexus for the galactic core. Self-maintaining via Architect systems no one fully understands.

### Terra / Sol System
Solari homeworld. Population ~11 billion. Hegemony capital. Considered by Solari to be the galaxy's beating heart; considered by other species to be a loud newcomer that hasn't yet learned to be quiet.

### Syrath Prime
Syrathi homeworld. A warm, high-humidity planet. Home to the Resonance Academies and most Elder Syrathi philosophical institutions. Considered the Compact's cultural capital.

### Vael
Vaekar homeworld. Harsh, high-gravity. Produced the most physically robust major species in the Compact. The Hierarchy's military academies are here.

### Keth'Sa
Keth homeworld. The site of the Gate Incident. Heavily rebuilt. The Keth Data Union's primary server infrastructure is buried 3km below the surface.

### Dur'ka
Durakh homeworld. Heavily irradiated following orbital bombardment during the Durakh Campaigns. Still inhabited — Durakh biology is radiation-tolerant. A planet of ruins, clan-fortresses, and salvage.

### The Wandering Armada
Not a location but a mobile fleet-nation. ~50,000 ships of wildly varying design, perpetually moving through Compact and border space, seeking resources and safe harbours.

### The Spire's Foundry District
The Spire's equivalent of Omega — an unregulated district deep in the station's old industrial sections where Accord law is technically enforced and practically ignored. Major hub for grey-market trade, mercenary hiring, and species that prefer not to be found.

### The Veil Expanse
Uncharted space beyond the galactic rim. Named for the dense nebular formations that make navigation and sensor readings difficult. Hollow entities have been detected here. The Compact officially discourages exploration; independent operators ignore this.

### The Null Rift
An anomalous region of space near the galactic core with unusually high Null-Matter concentration. A number of strange phenomena have been reported here. The Reclamation believes it is the location of the Architects' last known installation.
